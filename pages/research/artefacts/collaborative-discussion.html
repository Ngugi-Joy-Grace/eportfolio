Research Methods & Professional Practices

Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) (N.D.) Ethics Case Study

I analysed the Abusive Workplace Behaviour case study (ACM, N.D.) based on the violation of the following ACM code of ethics (2018): Principle 1.1 "Contribute to society and to human well-being", Principle 1.2 "Avoid harm, Principle 1.4 "Be fair and take action not to discriminate", and Principle 3.4 "Articulate, apply, and support policies and processes that reflect the principles of the Code" as well as the following British Computer Society (BCS) Code of Conduct (2022): Public Interest, Professional Competence and Integrity, and Duty to the Profession.

Feedback (Peer Response)

One peer pointed out the use of papers that predate the BCS and ACM adoption, while another prompted a discussion on whether the loss of membership has been an effective deterrent to abuse in the workplace.

Reflection

Overall, this discussion expanded my knowledge of the BCS Code of Conduct and the ACM Code of Ethics, which I was unfamiliar with before, and offered an opportunity to give and receive constructive feedback.
