Sentiment Analysis in a Multilingual Context with a Focus on Swahili

The feedback highlighted that the research and investigation were thorough and that my content was easy to follow. However, I did go over the 15-minute limit, extending to 23 minutes, which displayed my inability to present information concisely. Picking up on this, I plan to practice ways to simplify and reduce my content within a straightforward framework that is easy to follow and not riddled with technical jargon. Additionally, the tutor pointed out that my oral presentation could have used a little more intonation to enhance engagement.

From this feedback, I realise that for future presentation-themed assignments, I need to streamline my content by focusing on the core message. This will help me be precise and avoid exceeding the time limit (GVSU, 2016). Despite the assignment being a recording, I need to find ways to engage my audience, particularly through my delivery skills, by using intonation to emphasise key points (Jimenez, N.D.). I plan to practice in front of an audience before recording, or if thatâ€™s not possible, I will do multiple takes and have different people listen to it and provide feedback. This approach will ensure that my presentations are both concise and engaging.
