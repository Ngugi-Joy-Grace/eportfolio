Deciphering Big Data

1. Introduction

In this unit, the coding exercises delve deeply into data wrangling techniques as elaborated by Kazil and Jarmul (2016) in their book on Data Wrangling. Specifically, chapters 4 and 5 were explored, which provide a comprehensive guide on manipulating and extracting data from PDF and Excel files using Python.

Chapter 4 puts the focus on working with Excel files. Excel, being one of the most widely used data storage formats, presents its own unique set of challenges and opportunities for data wranglers. In this chapter, Kazil and Jarmul (2016) introduce the xlrd library for Python, which facilitates both reading from and writing to Excel files.

Chapter 5 provides a detailed walkthrough on parsing PDF files. This chapter explores the challenges often faced while working with PDFs, given their fixed-layout format. Kazil and Jarmul (2016) offer insights into various Python libraries such as slate3k and PDFTables, explaining their strengths and use cases.

It was however noted that xlrd no longer supports .xlsx files and therefore an alternative was used i.e., openpyxl. During research more libraries were also found as being useful in further work for PDF parsing e.g. tabula, PyPDF2 and camelot

5. Compliance

Unit 8: Compliance and Regulatory Framework for Managing Data was covered for this section

Tasks:
  • Comparing Compliance Laws against the GDPR
Take away:

This unit provided a comprehensive understanding of the GDPR's overarching principles and its implications for organisations and individuals. Particularly illuminating was the comparative analysis between GDPR and other global data protection acts,such as Kenya's Data Protection Act (DPA), Colombian Data Protection Law (DPL) and
UK's Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), which highlighted the nuances and commonalities across different legal frameworks. Such comparisons not only underlined the global shift towards more stringent data protection measures but also emphasised the need for organisations and data professionals to be agile and informed to navigate this multifaceted legal landscape.

7. Future

This covered work from Unit 12: Future of Big Data Analytics

Tasks:
  • Reflective piece
  • Content Challenge
Take away:

The future of big data analytics is set to be transformative, reshaping industries, enhancing decision-making, and unlocking new possibilities across various sectors. As computational capacities expand and algorithms become more sophisticated, the ability to process and interpret big datasets in real-time will revolutionise personalised medicine, predictive maintenance, smart cities, and more. The integration artificial intelligence and machine learning with big data analytics promises to yield insights that were previously inconceivable, enabling enhanced forecasting. A good example of this is the recent wave of generative AI models and large language models created to solve different sets of problems. However, with this surge in data-driven decision-making, the ethical implications concerning data privacy, security, and governance will become increasingly paramount, necessitating rigorous frameworks and standards to ensure responsible and transparent utilisation of big data.
